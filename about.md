## Inspiration
[cite_start]In hyper-dense Indian metropolises, platform-based delivery partners (like Zomato) are the backbone of the digital economy[cite: 9, 82]. [cite_start]However, every monsoon season, severe urban flooding paralyzes city sections, causing gig workers to lose 20-30% of their monthly earnings[cite: 10]. [cite_start]Traditional insurance only covers health or vehicle damage, completely ignoring the immediate loss of daily wages[cite: 11, 84]. [cite_start]We were inspired to build an AI-powered safety net that protects their actual earning power against these uncontrollable external events[cite: 14].

## How we built our project
[cite_start]We designed an event-driven parametric architecture focusing purely on Loss of Income[cite: 83]. [cite_start]It utilizes machine learning for dynamic pricing, structured strictly on a Weekly basis to match the gig worker payout cycle[cite: 18, 85].

[cite_start]To secure the liquidity pool against organized GPS-spoofing syndicates—directly addressing the 24-hour Market Crash compliance challenge [cite: 183, 184]—we integrated edge-level telemetry (`isMock()` APIs) and physical sensor fusion. By extracting the device's triaxial acceleration vectors, we mathematically derive physical velocity to cross-reference kinetic reality with GPS claims using:

$$v = \int a \, dt$$

Furthermore, we designed a Graph Neural Network (GNN) to map claims as nodes, identifying fraudulent syndicates sharing identical hardware IDs. To prioritize massive structural anomalies and isolate organized fraud rings, we evaluate data segments using a custom mathematical fitness function:

$$fitness = \frac{abs\_anom\_amt \times rel\_amt}{level^{1.2}}$$

## The challenges we faced
Our biggest challenge was balancing strict adversarial defense with a fair user experience. A strict anti-spoofing system risks penalizing honest workers who simply lose their network connection during a severe storm. We solved this by designing a soft-degradation workflow featuring offline telemetry caching, where sensor data is locally encrypted and uploaded for validation once the network is restored.
